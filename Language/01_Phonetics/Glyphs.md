# Lingua Sona's Glyphs
## What Are They?
The glyphs of Lingua Sona are used to form its [[Letters]]. There are just over 30 glyphs which through the learning of which allows the construction of every letter used within Lingua Sona. Each glyph represents a partial of a phonetic sound such as a consonant, vowel, vowel cluster, or a few special symbols. 

Mostly, you will find that most letters use two glyphs. One for the consonant end and one for the vowel end. By combining the two using a imaginary middle point, the number of strokes necessary to write Lingua Sona's letters is decreased. Thus, not only do glyphs make the letters much easier to learn, but so do too, do they make it faster to write.

By the time you are reading this Markdown, the glyph designs are likely finalized and you can view all of them within the `./Glyphs/` folder.
