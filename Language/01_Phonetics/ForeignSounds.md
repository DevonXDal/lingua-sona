# Foreign Sounds in Proper Nouns

## Introduction and Method
Likely, you'll find that at points, a proper noun of some form of foreign nature is without representation in Lingua Sona. To get around this, drawing a line vertically up and right back down to the center point symbolizes that the consonant uses a foreign but similar pronunciation. For a vowel, you simply draw a line vertically down and right back up to the center point. In some cases, you may need to do both and that is fine. The vertical line need not be perfect by any means, and a little loop to curve back down/up can be done if desired to avoid lifting the writing utensil.

## Examples of Consonants and Vowels with Foreign Counterparts
- F -> V
- J/Y -> J as used in English. 
- W -> R with the rolling r sound that drives it.
- K -> Q with a "kw-" sound
- Z -> X with a "zy-" sound

## Important Rule
*Foreign sound markers never change sorting or base identity, only pronunciation.*
